# Nissa — Ideal Customer Profile

## Primary ICP

### Company Profile
- **Size:** 100-1,000 employees
- **Revenue:** $50M-$500M
- **Type:** Mid-market, US-based
- **Procurement model:** Finance-led (no dedicated procurement software yet, or CLM exists but underused)
- **Contract volume:** 50-1,000 active vendor contracts
- **Industries with highest pain:** SaaS/technology, professional services, healthcare, manufacturing, PE-backed portfolio companies

### Buyer Titles (Decision Makers)
| Title | Role in Deal | What They Care About |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| **CFO** | Final sign-off, budget holder | Cost savings, visibility into vendor spend, renewal risk |
| **VP Finance** | Champion, day-to-day owner | Efficiency, reducing manual contract review, reporting |
| **VP/Head of Procurement** | Champion or evaluator | Policy enforcement, vendor benchmarking, negotiation leverage |
| **Director of Operations** | Influencer | Process improvement, reducing contract chaos |

### Influencers
- General Counsel / Legal (must not feel threatened)
- IT (security review)
- Department heads who manage vendor relationships

## Five Target Segments

### 1. First CLM Candidates
- **Profile:** No CLM today. Contracts live in email, shared drives, filing cabinets
- **Trigger:** Growing fast, contracts getting out of control, missed a renewal
- **Why Nissa wins:** Zero migration. Forward an email and you're started. No IT project.
- **Messaging:** "You don't need a CLM. You need your contracts to be findable and useful."

### 2. CLM Exists But Underused
- **Profile:** Bought Ironclad/Juro/etc., adoption is 20-30%, most contracts still live in email
- **Trigger:** Frustrated with ROI on existing CLM, renewal coming up
- **Why Nissa wins:** Works alongside or replaces their CLM without another migration
- **Messaging:** "Your team didn't fail your CLM. Your CLM failed your team."

### 3. Renewal-Heavy / PE-Backed
- **Profile:** PE portfolio company or subscription-heavy business with 100+ vendor contracts
- **Trigger:** PE firm wants visibility into vendor spend across portfolio, or company is bleeding money on auto-renewals
- **Why Nissa wins:** Immediate savings identification. Renewal tracking from day one.
- **Messaging:** "You're losing 11% of contract value post-signature. We find it."

### 4. Procurement Team Just Forming
- **Profile:** Company just hired their first procurement lead or VP
- **Trigger:** New hire needs to get arms around existing contracts fast
- **Why Nissa wins:** Instant visibility without a 6-month implementation
- **Messaging:** "Get a handle on every contract in your portfolio this week, not next quarter."

### 5. Design Partners
- **Profile:** Forward-thinking finance/procurement leaders willing to co-build
- **Trigger:** Intellectually curious, frustrated with status quo, want early access
- **Why Nissa wins:** Shape the product, get preferred pricing, direct line to founders
- **Messaging:** "Help us build the contract tool you actually wish existed."

## Validated Pain Points

Based on ICP research (daily automated research since Feb 2026):

1. **"We don't know what contracts we have."** Basic visibility is missing at most mid-market companies
2. **"We got auto-renewed into a contract nobody wanted."** The #1 money-losing event in procurement
3. **"Our CLM is a $50K filing cabinet."** Adoption failure is the norm, not the exception
4. **"Contract review takes 2 weeks."** Legal bottleneck slows everything down
5. **"We can't benchmark vendor pricing."** No cross-contract intelligence means every negotiation starts from zero

## Validated Objections

| Objection | Reality | Response |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| "We've tried tools like this before and no one used them" | Adoption fails because tools require behavior change. Nissa doesn't. | "That's exactly why we built Nissa to work inside email and Slack. If your team can forward an email, they can use Nissa." |
| "Legal will block this / it'll take 6 months" | Legal blocks tools that touch contract creation. Nissa is post-signature intelligence. | "We're not touching your signing workflow. We're making contracts useful after they're signed. Legal loves that." |
| "Will this replace people?" | No. It makes procurement people more effective. | "Nissa replaces the spreadsheet your team hates maintaining, not the people." |
| "Our contracts are too complex" | Nissa handles complexity better than humans scanning PDFs | "Complex contracts are exactly where AI extraction outperforms manual review. The messier your portfolio, the more value Nissa delivers." |
| "We need SOC 2 / security review" | On the roadmap, not there yet | Be transparent. Offer data handling documentation. SOC 2 in progress. |

## Disqualification Criteria

Don't pursue if:
- Under 50 employees (not enough contract volume)
- Over 5,000 employees (enterprise sales cycle, need dedicated AE)
- Legal-led procurement (they'll want a full CLM, not lightweight)
- Under 20 active vendor contracts (not enough pain)
- Government / heavily regulated (compliance requirements exceed current capabilities)
