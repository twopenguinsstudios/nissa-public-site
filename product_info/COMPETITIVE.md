# Nissa — Competitive Landscape

## The Real Competitor

**It's not Ironclad. It's Excel and email.**

80%+ of mid-market companies manage contracts in shared drives, email threads, and spreadsheets. They don't have a CLM. They don't want a CLM. They want to find a contract when they need it and know when a renewal is coming.

Nissa's primary competition is the status quo. Every other competitor listed below is secondary.

## Competitor Map

### Tier 1: Enterprise CLMs (Not Direct Competitors, but Referenced)

#### Icertis
- **What they do:** Enterprise CLM platform, Fortune 500 focused
- **Deal size:** $500K-$2M+/yr
- **Setup:** 6-12 months
- **Why they're not a threat:** Different universe. If a prospect mentions Icertis, they're either too big for Nissa or they're scared of Icertis pricing and looking for an alternative.
- **Nissa angle:** "Icertis is built for companies with dedicated procurement departments and 7-figure budgets. Nissa is built for everyone else."

#### SAP Ariba / Coupa
- **What they do:** Full procurement suites with contract modules
- **Deal size:** $200K+/yr
- **Setup:** Months to years
- **Why they're not a threat:** Nissa isn't competing with procurement suites. These are system-of-record plays.
- **Nissa angle:** "If you're buying Ariba, you don't need us. If you're NOT buying Ariba, you probably need us."

### Tier 2: Modern CLMs (Primary Competitive References)

#### Ironclad
- **What they do:** Workflow-first CLM, strong in legal ops
- **Pricing:** ~$25K-$100K+/yr
- **Setup:** 2-6 months
- **Strengths:** Beautiful product, strong brand, good legal workflow
- **Weaknesses:** 
  - Pre-signature focused. Post-signature is an afterthought
  - Requires significant adoption effort
  - Pricing excludes mid-market
  - Implementation timeline kills urgency
- **Nissa angle:** "Ironclad is great if you want to optimize how contracts get signed. Nissa is what you need after they're signed."

#### Juro
- **What they do:** Modern, collaborative CLM
- **Pricing:** ~$15K-$50K/yr
- **Setup:** 1-3 months
- **Strengths:** Clean UI, good collaboration features, faster than Ironclad
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Still workflow-focused
  - Requires migration and adoption
  - Post-signature features are limited
  - Mid-market pricing but enterprise complexity
- **Nissa angle:** "Juro makes contracts look better. Nissa makes them work harder after they're signed."

#### ContractPodAI
- **What they do:** AI-powered CLM with legal AI assistant
- **Pricing:** $50K-$150K+/yr
- **Setup:** 3-6 months
- **Strengths:** Strong AI capabilities, legal focus
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Enterprise pricing and sales cycle
  - Heavy implementation
  - Overkill for mid-market
- **Nissa angle:** "Same AI intelligence, 10% of the cost and complexity."

### Tier 3: Adjacent Tools

#### SpendHQ / Spend Analytics
- **What they do:** Procurement spend analytics
- **Relationship:** Complementary, not competitive
- **Note:** SpendHQ analyzes spend data. Nissa analyzes contract terms. Different inputs, different outputs. Could be an integration partner.

#### DocuSign CLM (formerly Springcm)
- **What they do:** Contract lifecycle bolt-on to DocuSign
- **Weaknesses:** Clunky, feels like an afterthought to e-signature
- **Nissa angle:** "We integrate with DocuSign for signing. We do the thinking after."

#### PandaDoc / Proposify
- **What they do:** Proposal and document automation
- **Relationship:** Pre-signature tools. Not competitive with Nissa's post-signature focus.

### Tier 4: AI-First Newcomers (Watch List)

#### Lexion (acquired by Docusign)
- AI-native CLM, was closest to Nissa's positioning before acquisition
- Now absorbed into DocuSign's ecosystem
- Validates the market but removes an independent competitor

#### ThoughtRiver
- AI contract review, pre-signature focus
- Legal risk analysis, not post-signature intelligence
- Different use case

#### Evisort
- AI contract analytics
- Closer to Nissa's space but enterprise-focused
- Heavy implementation, not lightweight

## Nissa's Competitive Moat

### 1. Post-Signature Focus
Nobody else leads with this. Every competitor is optimizing for getting TO the signature. Nissa is the only platform built for what happens AFTER. That's a category-defining position.

### 2. Zero-Friction Ingestion
Email forward vs. file upload and migration. The adoption barrier is essentially zero. This isn't a feature, it's a fundamental architecture decision that competitors can't retrofit.

### 3. Speed to Value
Live in under a week vs. months. First analysis in 60 seconds vs. weeks of setup. This compresses the sales cycle and eliminates implementation risk.

### 4. Mid-Market Pricing
$5K-$24K/yr puts Nissa in a budget range that doesn't require board approval at most mid-market companies. Competitors at $50K+ trigger a completely different buying process.

### 5. AI-Native Architecture
Built on AI from day one, not bolted onto a legacy platform. This means faster iteration, better extraction accuracy over time, and features that aren't possible with traditional CLM architecture.

## Competitive Battlecard

### When prospect mentions Ironclad/Juro:
> "Great tools for legal workflow. But what happens after the contract is signed? That's where 90% of the value lives, and where those tools stop. Nissa starts there."

### When prospect says "we already have a CLM":
> "How's adoption? If your answer is 'not great,' you're not alone. 70%+ of CLMs underperform because they require behavior change. Nissa works inside email and Slack. Nothing to adopt."

### When prospect says "we use spreadsheets":
> "You and 80% of mid-market companies. That works until you miss an auto-renewal or can't find a contract during a negotiation. Nissa is the upgrade from spreadsheets that doesn't feel like a 6-month IT project."

### When prospect asks about security:
> "Contract data is segregated per customer and encrypted. We don't train AI on your data. SOC 2 certification is on our roadmap. Happy to walk through our data handling practices."

### When prospect asks about AI accuracy:
> "Nissa surfaces terms and benchmarks. It doesn't make legal decisions. Think of it as giving your team instant access to what's in the contract so they can make better decisions faster. Procurement stays in control."
